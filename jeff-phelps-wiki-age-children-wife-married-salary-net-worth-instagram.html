Jeff Phelps Biography – Jeff Phelps Wiki

Jeff Phelps is an American broadcast journalist. He is a sports Broadcaster at Fox Sports Ohio, United States. Phelps serves as the television pregame and postgame host for Cleveland Cavaliers telecasts on Fox Sports Ohio. He is also the pregame and postgame host for Cleveland Browns radio broadcasts, and also the midday co-host on WKRK-FM (92.3 The Fan) in Cleveland. Phelps started his journalism career in Akron on co-owned WAKR AM 1590 and WAKR-TV 23, as a sports reporter. He then had a five-year term as a sportscaster on KJAC in Beaumont, Texas.

In 1988, Phelps started working as a sports reporter and weekend sports anchor for WUAB Channel 43 in Cleveland, on the 10 O’Clock News. He was also serving as WOIO channel 19 after it had become WUAB’s sister station. Phelps then becoming the weeknight sports anchor for both stations. In 1994, Phelps was awarded for his work by the National Sportscasters and Sportswriters Association (NSSA) and named the 1994 Ohio Sportscaster of the Year.

Phelps later moved to Fox Sports Ohio where he became the pregame and postgame host for Cleveland Cavalier’s telecasts, in 2003. In 2011, he became the co-host for the midday Baskin & Phelps program, alongside former WEWS-TV 5 sports director Andy Baskin. Phelps continues his Fox Sports Ohio work along with his radio duties.

Phelps is the recipient of several awards including the Three-time Lower Great Lakes Emmy Awards recipient in 1991, 1993, and 1999. In 1994, he was recognized as the Ohio Sportscaster of the Year, presented by NSSA.
Phelps was born in Akron Ohio, United States. He attended Kent State University and graduated in the class of 1982.
